## Anatomy

`GlassToastContainer` is mounted once at the app root and takes no props. Each
toast is a floating glass card with a variant-coloured left border, the message
text, and a dismiss affordance. Stacking, enter, exit, and reflow are driven by
a spring; toasts are pushed through the `toast()` store, not as children.

## Usage

- **Do** — mount one container at the root and fire toasts through `toast.success()` / `toast.error()` for confirmations and recoverable failures.
- **Don't** — use a toast for content the user must act on or read carefully; it auto-dismisses and won't survive a glance away.

## Accessibility

- The container is an `aria-live="polite"` `role="status"` region — new toasts are announced without stealing focus.
- For urgent, error-class messages, escalate the live region to `assertive` so it interrupts.
- Every toast carries a labelled dismiss button (`aria-label="Dismiss"`) so it never depends on the auto-timeout alone.
- Use the **Accessibility** tab on any story canvas for the live axe audit.
